Emergencies

In case of a medical emergency, Student Health Services and/or the University Police will help in managing the emergency quickly and effectively. Call the Boston University Police at 617-353-2121 (3-2121 on a campus phone) or 911 if you have a life-threatening emergency. True medical emergencies are transported by ambulance to local hospital emergency departments. Boston University Student Health Services will call to follow up on your emergency and will help in arranging additional care when needed.

Help Info

  • Helping Students in Distress

    • Life at Boston University can be very stressful for students as they face the challenge of a highly competitive academic environment. And begin to assume a variety of roles and responsibilities. Therefore many students come to the University with struggles that challenge their ability to succeed in this environment.

    Faculty, staff, and other students may witness early signs of distress as they played out on campus, in classroom, and within residence halls. Students can seek assistance from faculty, staff, and other students, if they are willing to listen. And there are also professional services available to students on campus through the Dean of Students and Behavioral Medicine.


  • Identifying Distress

    • Students can check the academic signs and physical or psychological signs to identify whether they are in Distress.



  • For Students

    • Students can follow the simple guidelines and also make an appointment to Students Health Services and Behavioral Medicine, and both are at 881 Commonwealth Avenue West.

    Behavioral Medicine

  • Behavioral Medicine Services

    • Behavioral Medicine staff, psychiatrists, clinical nurse specialists, licensed mental health clinicians and licensed clinical social workers, are available to address the many psychological and adjustment issues that arise in a university community.

    Appointments or urgent care are available by calling 617-353-3569, Behavioral Medicine clinicians are available 24 hours a day on-call for mental health emergencies. Faculty and staff may contact Behavioral Medicine at 617-353-3569 if they are concerned about a student. If there are concerns of immediate danger, please contact BUPD (http://www.bu.edu/police/) directly.

    More information about ADHD, Eating Disorders, Stress, Anxiety, Depression, Sleep Issues, Academic and Adjustment Issues, Alcohol and other health issues can be found on http://www.bu.edu/shs/resources/ .


  • Behavioral Medicine Groups

    • An array of groups, workshops, and other resources are provided to support students. All BU students are welcome to attend groups and workshops. To sign up, please directly contact Behavioral Medicine at 617-353-3569.


  • Sexual Assault Response and Prevention

    • What We Do

      • Sexual Assault Response and Prevention Center provide rapid, confidential, compassionate, supportive, and free-of-charge advocacy and assistance to BU students who have experienced a traumatic incident. This includes, but is not limited to, sexual assault, physical assault, interpersonal violence, and other crimes.

    • Confidentiality

      • Sexual Assault Response and Prevention Center are bound by law to maintain your complete confidentiality. You will not be pressured to make decisions; rather, you will be given information and support to make decisions that benefit you both now and in the future. We can help you regain control after control of something vital has been taken away.

    • Hours & Availability

      • Our counselors rotate being on call 24 hours a day, to respond to urgent situations. You can reach us by calling 617-353-SARP (7277).Counseling appointments can be scheduled when the office is open, Monday–Friday,9 a.m.–5 p.m.
      When calling after 5pm, on a weekend or holiday, you will hear a message saying you have reached SARP. Then there will be a brief pause followed by a prompt to press 1 to leave a non-urgent voice message or press 2 to speak with a Crisis Counselor.

    Insurance & Billing

  • Identifying Distress

    • Boston University offers a Student Health Insurance Plan, which provides excellent coverage for most medical and mental health problems that college students encounter. Visit the Aetna Student Health website (https://www.aetnastudenthealth.com/) for more information. For updates related to the Affordable Care Act (ACA) (https://www.aetnastudenthealth.com/schools/acasite/index.html) visit Aetna’s new website, What Do I Really Need to Know, which provides videos, interactive tools, and information to help students navigate their health insurance options.


  • Students Who Enroll in the University Insurance Plan

    • The University’s insurance plan (Aetna Student Health), like most insurance plans, may not pay for all of your care. Please review your benefits to determine what services are covered. This information is available at the Aetna Student Health website. If you are enrolled in the University’s insurance plan through Aetna Student Health, most of your services provided at Student Health Services will be billed to your plan automatically.


  • Students Who Do Not Enroll in the University Insurance Plan

    • Students are financially responsible for all services outside Student Health Services and for all lab fees and immunizations at Student Health Services. If you are being referred to a specialist for an X-ray or for any other services outside Student Health Services and you waived the University’s insurance plan (Aetna Student Health), call your insurance company or your parents to find out whether your insurance will pay for it. If your insurance will not cover the cost of this care, you will be responsible for these bills.


  • Charges for Common Services

    • Billing information about Basic Plan – Aetna Student Health, Plus Plan – Aetna Student Health, Waived – Third Party Insurance. And other charge fee about common lab tests, orthopedic equipment, vaccines/immunizations, women’s health. More information on http://www.bu.edu/shs/fees/common-services/
